# 📊 Revenue Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Objective
Analyze revenue performance and identify the key drivers behind it.

---

## 🛠 Tools Used
- SQL (data analysis)
- Power BI (dashboard & visualization)

---

## 📂 Dataset
The dataset includes:
- Orders (revenue, product, customer)
- Customers (region)
- Products (brand, category)
- Date table (time analysis)

---

## 📈 Key Business Question
How is revenue performing, and what drives it?  
Is revenue driven by premium brands or mass market?

---

## 🔍 Analysis Summary

### 1. Revenue Trend
- Revenue remains relatively stable over time with moderate fluctuations
- No extreme spikes or drops

---

### 2. Brand Performance
- Revenue is highly concentrated in two brands: Rolex and Casio (~74%)
- Rolex (premium) leads through high-value sales
- Casio (mass market) performs strongly through volume

---

### 3. Product Category
- Revenue is evenly distributed across categories
- No single category dominates performance

---

### 4. Regional Performance
- Asia generates the highest revenue
- Europe and North America follow closely

---

### 5. Customer Analysis
- Revenue is highly diversified across customers
- No single customer contributes significantly
- Indicates low dependency risk

---

## 🧠 Conclusion
Revenue is primarily driven by brand positioning.  
Both premium and mass-market strategies contribute significantly, with revenue concentrated in a few key brands rather than product categories or individual customers.
